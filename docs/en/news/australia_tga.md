### FDA and Australia's TGA Sign Confidentiality Commitment to Share Non-Public Information on Medical Devices and Other Products

**2026-07-01** | Australia TGA | Safety Communication | !! MEDIUM

On January 11, 2018, the U.S. FDA and Australia's Therapeutic Goods Administration (TGA) finalized a Confidentiality Commitment that permits the exchange of trade secrets and commercial confidential information regarding medical devices, drugs, food, and other regulated products. This arrangement supports joint regulatory activities such as recalls, market withdrawals, and safety alerts while safeguarding sensitive data. Medical device manufacturers should be aware that confidential information submitted to one agency may be shared with the other under this commitment to enhance post-market surveillance and public health protection.

### FDA Releases Updated Medical Device Single Audit Program (MDSAP) Overview

**2026-07-01** | Australia TGA | Regulation Update | !! MEDIUM

The U.S. FDA has updated its webpage for the Medical Device Single Audit Program (MDSAP), which allows a single regulatory audit by an MDSAP-recognized Auditing Organization to satisfy requirements of multiple jurisdictions. This update clarifies how manufacturers selling devices in participating countries (U.S., Canada, Japan, Brazil, Australia) can leverage MDSAP audits to streamline compliance and potentially replace routine FDA inspections. Manufacturers should review the revised content for current program details and enrollment guidance.
