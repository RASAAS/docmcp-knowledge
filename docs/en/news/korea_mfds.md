### FDA Current Good Manufacturing Practice (CGMP) Regulations for Pharmaceutical Products

**2026-07-01** | Korea MFDS | Regulation Update | !! MEDIUM

The FDA's CGMP regulations establish minimum quality standards for the methods, facilities, and controls used in manufacturing, processing, and packing of drug products. All pharmaceutical manufacturers, including those of finished dosage forms and active pharmaceutical ingredients, must comply to ensure product safety, identity, strength, and purity. Key requirements include robust quality systems, process validation, thorough documentation, and investigation of deviations. Failure to meet CGMP can result in product being deemed adulterated and subject to regulatory enforcement.

### FDA Finalizes Quality Management System Regulation (QMSR) Harmonizing with ISO 13485:2016

**2026-07-01** | Korea MFDS | Regulation Update | !!! HIGH

The FDA has issued the final Quality Management System Regulation (QMSR), amending 21 CFR Part 820 to align device CGMP requirements with ISO 13485:2016. This rule affects all medical device manufacturers and requires them to establish a quality management system consistent with the international standard. Key compliance activities include updating quality policies, design controls, risk management, and supplier management processes. The regulation becomes effective on February 2, 2026, with a transition period for implementation.

### FDA's Quality Management System Regulation (QMSR) Becomes Effective on February 2, 2026

**2026-07-01** | Korea MFDS | Regulation Update | !!! HIGH

The FDA's Quality Management System Regulation (QMSR) took effect on February 2, 2026, amending the current good manufacturing practice (CGMP) requirements for medical devices. This regulation aligns the FDA's quality system framework with ISO 13485:2016, affecting all medical device manufacturers subject to the QS regulation. Manufacturers must now ensure their quality management systems comply with the QMSR, replacing the previous Quality System (QS) regulation. The change aims to harmonize international standards and reduce regulatory burden.

### FDA Modernization of Cosmetics Regulation Act (MoCRA) Overview and Key Requirements

**2026-07-01** | Korea MFDS | Regulation Update | !!! HIGH

The Modernization of Cosmetics Regulation Act of 2022 (MoCRA) significantly expands FDA’s authority over cosmetics, introducing mandatory recall power, facility registration, product listing, and adverse event reporting. Cosmetic manufacturers, packers, and distributors must register facilities, list products with the FDA, and report serious adverse events within 15 days. The law also requires safety substantiation of products and maintenance of records, marking a major shift toward proactive cosmetics oversight.

[View Source (FDA)](https://www.fda.gov/cosmetics/cosmetics-laws-regulations/modernization-cosmetics-regulation-act-2022-mocra)
