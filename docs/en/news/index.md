# Regulatory News

> Latest regulatory updates, standard revisions, and guidance publications in the medical device compliance space.

### ISO 14971:2024 Amendment 1 -- Risk Management for Medical Devices

**2025-06-01** | Shared Standards | Standard Revision | !!! HIGH

ISO published Amendment 1 to ISO 14971:2019, addressing alignment with the latest EU MDR requirements and clarifying risk acceptability criteria. The amendment introduces updated guidance on benefit-risk analysis methodology and integration with post-market surveillance data. Transition period ends December 2026.

**Tags**: `ISO`, `Risk Management`, `ISO 14971`

[View Source (ISO)](https://www.iso.org/standard/72704.html)

### NMPA Published Revised Classification Catalogue for Medical Devices (2025 Edition)

**2025-05-01** | NMPA | Regulation Update | !!! HIGH

The National Medical Products Administration (NMPA) published the revised Medical Device Classification Catalogue (2025 Edition), effective from January 1, 2026. Key changes include updated classification rules for AI-assisted diagnostic software, new entries for combination products, and revised risk classifications for several device categories. Manufacturers should review product registrations against the new catalogue.

**Tags**: `NMPA`, `Classification`, `Catalogue`

[View Source (NMPA)](https://www.nmpa.gov.cn/)

### MDCG 2025-9: Guidance on Clinical Evaluation Consultation Procedure (Article 54)

**2025-04-01** | EU MDR/IVDR | New Guidance | !! MEDIUM

The Medical Device Coordination Group (MDCG) published new guidance document MDCG 2025-9, providing detailed instructions on the clinical evaluation consultation procedure under Article 54 of the MDR. The guidance clarifies the roles of expert panels, timelines for assessment, and documentation requirements for Class III and Class IIb implantable devices.

**Tags**: `MDR`, `MDCG`, `Clinical Evaluation`, `Article 54`

[View Source (EC MDCG)](https://health.ec.europa.eu/medical-devices-sector/new-regulations/guidance-mdcg-endorsed-documents-and-other-guidance_en)

### FDA Issues Final Guidance on Predetermined Change Control Plans for AI/ML-Enabled Devices

**2025-03-15** | FDA | New Guidance | !!! HIGH

The FDA issued final guidance on Predetermined Change Control Plans (PCCPs) for machine learning-enabled device software functions. The guidance outlines how manufacturers can describe planned modifications and the methodology for implementing those changes in a controlled manner, without requiring a new premarket submission for each change. This is a significant step in the FDA's framework for regulating adaptive AI/ML-based Software as a Medical Device (SaMD).

**Tags**: `FDA`, `AI/ML`, `SaMD`, `PCCP`, `510(k)`

[View Source (FDA CDRH)](https://www.fda.gov/regulatory-information/search-fda-guidance-documents)

### Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2025/425 -- Updated Harmonised Standards for MDR

**2025-03-03** | EU MDR/IVDR | Regulation Update | !!! HIGH

The European Commission published implementing decision (EU) 2025/425 on 3 March 2025, updating the list of harmonised standards for medical devices under Regulation (EU) 2017/745. Key changes include new references for EN ISO 10993 series (biocompatibility), EN ISO 14971:2019/A11:2021 (risk management), and updated IEC 62304 (software lifecycle). Manufacturers should review their declarations of conformity for compliance with newly cited standards.

**Tags**: `MDR`, `Harmonised Standards`, `CID`

[View Source (EUR-Lex)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/dec_impl/2025/425/oj)
